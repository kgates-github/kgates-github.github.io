How do you think about technology as part of the design process?
When designing, I think about technology in terms of technical possibilities and the cost/value ratio of solutions. I also consider how to reduce the tax on future possibilities to benefit the user in the long run. I also try to collaborate with engineers to get their feedback early and understand their perspective.

What is an example of when things didn't go as planned?
In short, our solution ideas were not solid enough technically for our engineers. For a lot of types of projects, safe assumptions can be made about the tech. That was not the case for us. We went back to the whiteboard and discussed. The solution was to group ideas into product concepts. Those allowed the engineers to envision the "how" to a level that was acceptable.
